Working in IT Helpdesk gave me a strong foundation in troubleshooting, user support, and system reliability.  
Over time, I realized that solving individual issues was only part of the bigger picture — the real challenge lies in maintaining stable infrastructure behind the scenes.

This post shares my journey from Helpdesk to IT Infrastructure.

---

## Starting with Technical Support

My early experience in IT Technical Support involved:

- Diagnosing hardware and software issues  
- Supporting end users onsite  
- Handling network-related troubleshooting  
- Managing user accounts and system access  

This phase helped me build structured problem-solving skills and strong communication with non-technical users.

---

## Moving Toward Infrastructure

While working at SanAn Corporation Vietnam, I gradually became more involved in:

- System monitoring  
- Infrastructure stability  
- Internal technical operations  
- Preventive maintenance  

Instead of reacting to issues, I began focusing on identifying root causes and preventing recurring incidents.

---

## Core Skills I Developed

Through hands-on experience, I strengthened my capabilities in:

- System Monitoring  
- Linux fundamentals  
- Windows administration  
- Networking fundamentals  
- Technical troubleshooting  
- User & access management  

These skills helped me understand how front-line support connects with backend infrastructure.

---

## Certification & Continuous Learning

To support my growth, I earned the **OCNA (Oracle Certified Network Associate)** certification.

Currently, I am expanding my knowledge in:

- Automation (PowerShell / Bash)  
- Advanced Linux concepts  
- Infrastructure reliability practices  

---

## What’s Next?

My long-term goal is to deepen my expertise in infrastructure and system operations.  
I aim to contribute not just as a support engineer, but as someone who improves overall IT reliability and operational efficiency.
