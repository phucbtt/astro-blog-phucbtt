Every IT professional starts somewhere.  
For me, it began with technical support and troubleshooting — solving day-to-day user issues and keeping systems running.

This blog is where I document that journey.

---

## From Troubleshooting to System Thinking

Working in IT Helpdesk taught me how to:

- Diagnose hardware and software issues  
- Support end users under pressure  
- Communicate technical solutions clearly  
- Think logically when systems fail  

But over time, I realized something important:

Fixing issues is reactive.  
Building stable systems is proactive.

That realization pushed me to learn more about infrastructure, system monitoring, and backend operations.

---

## Why Document the Journey?

Technology changes fast. Writing helps me:

- Reinforce what I learn  
- Clarify technical concepts  
- Build structured thinking  
- Track my professional growth  

This blog is not just about sharing knowledge — it is about building discipline in continuous learning.

---

## What You Can Expect Here

I plan to write about:

- Real troubleshooting cases  
- Linux and Windows fundamentals  
- Networking basics for IT support  
- System monitoring concepts  
- Career growth in IT Infrastructure  

Some posts will be beginner-friendly. Others will be deeper technical breakdowns.

---

## Long-Term Vision

My long-term goal is to grow from Helpdesk toward a stronger Infrastructure-focused role.

Not just resolving incidents —  
but designing and maintaining reliable systems.

---

If you are also working in IT Support or transitioning toward Infrastructure, I hope this blog brings practical insights and motivation.

Thanks for reading. More technical posts coming soon.
